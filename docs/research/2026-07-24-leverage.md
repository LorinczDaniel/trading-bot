# Leverage on BTC: measured, and it goes the wrong way

**Date:** 2026-07-24
**Question:** the only positive-Sharpe thing found all session was holding BTC. Does
leverage make it better?

**Answer: no. On this price path, leverage above 1x produced *lower* returns and
*higher* drawdowns.** Not a risk/return trade — strictly worse on both.

Constant leverage, rebalanced daily (how a perp position actually behaves), maintenance
margin 0.5%, BTC/USDT daily 2017-08-17 → 2026-07-24.

## Result 1 — Sharpe is invariant, exactly as theory says

| leverage | return | max drawdown | Sharpe | outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.0x | **+1417.7%** | −83.2% | 0.80 | survived |
| 1.5x | +1087.8% | −94.9% | 0.80 | survived |
| 2.0x | **+162.1%** | −99.1% | 0.80 | survived |
| 3.0x | −100.0% | −100.0% | — | **LIQUIDATED 2020-03-12** |
| 5.0x | −100.0% | −100.0% | — | **LIQUIDATED 2020-03-12** |
| 10.0x | −100.0% | −100.0% | — | **LIQUIDATED 2017-09-14** |

Sharpe is **0.80 at every surviving leverage**. That is the whole point: leverage
multiplies return and volatility by the same factor, so it cannot manufacture edge. It
only changes the size of the bet — and adds a failure mode that unlevered holding does not
have.

## Result 2 — volatility drag, and it is enormous

Leverage is not a multiplier on total return. Daily rebalancing means compounding a
levered series, and variance drains it:

| | naive expectation | actual | drag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5x | +2126.6% | +1087.8% | **−1038.8pp** |
| 2.0x | +2835.5% | +162.1% | **−2673.3pp** |

**2x leverage on an asset that rose 15x returned +162%.** Unlevered holding returned
+1417.7%. The drag scales with the square of volatility, and BTC's daily volatility is
~3.5%, so at 2x it costs roughly 0.12% per day — about 35% a year, compounded for nine
years.

This is the counterintuitive part and it is not an opinion: on a high-volatility asset,
leverage is a structurally different and worse bet, not "more of the same."

## Result 3 — liquidation is a path property, not an average

3x and 5x both died on **2020-03-12**, the COVID crash, when BTC fell roughly half in a
day. 10x died in its first month.

Across every 2-year start window:

| leverage | windows | liquidated | % killed | median return |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5x | 85 | 0 | 0.0% | +108.5% |
| 2.0x | 85 | 0 | 0.0% | +51.6% |
| 3.0x | 85 | 25 | **29.4%** | **−91.2%** |
| 5.0x | 85 | 25 | 29.4% | −100.0% |

Nearly a third of 2-year windows at 3x ended in total loss, and the median outcome was
−91.2%. That median is the number that matters: it is not a tail risk, it is the typical
result.

## Result 4 — funding makes it worse

Holding a levered long on a perp costs funding. At the rate measured live (0.000051 per
8h ≈ 5.6% annualized on the borrowed portion):

| leverage | without funding | with funding |
|---|---|---|
| 1.5x | +1087.8% | +825.2% |
| 2.0x | +162.1% | **+58.9%** |

## Where leverage *is* the right tool

None of the above says leverage is useless. It says leverage is wrong **here**.

The drag scales with σ², so on a low-volatility strategy it costs almost nothing. That is
precisely why basis traders and volatility sellers use it: a cash-and-carry position
yielding ~7% a year with near-zero directional exposure is not worth trading unlevered,
and levering it does not incur meaningful drag.

So leverage belongs on strategies that are **positive-Sharpe and low-volatility**. We do
not have one. The two candidates that could qualify are the premium trades already
identified and still untested — cash-and-carry basis and the variance risk premium — and
the harvested research already flags the catch there: at 10x, the basis trade **would have
been liquidated in more than half of all months in 2018–2024**, because the spot and
futures legs are not cross-margined.

That is the same lesson as Result 3, in the place it would actually apply.

## Practical note

The bot has no leverage support. It is spot-only by design: `LiveBroker` places spot
market orders, and `reconcile.py` and the risk manager assume an unlevered book. Adding
leverage means the futures API, margin management, and liquidation monitoring — a
significant change to the live path, not a parameter.

## Conclusion

On BTC directional exposure, the measured answer is that **1x dominates every leverage
tested**: higher return than 1.5x and 2x, no liquidation risk, and identical Sharpe. There
is no configuration in this data where leverage improved the outcome.

Leverage is a position-sizing decision, not a strategy. It cannot create an edge that is
not there, and applied to a volatile asset it destroys returns it appears to multiply.
